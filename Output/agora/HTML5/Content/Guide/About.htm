Nowy Sącz Tourist Guide

General information

Nowy Sącz is a town with county rights in Poland, located in Małopolskie Voivodeship (Lesser Poland Province), the seat of the Nowy Sącz Land County. The third largest city in the Lesser Poland Voivodeship and the fifth largest city in the Beskid Sądecki region, the capital of Sądecczyzna. One of the oldest towns in Lesser Poland - founded on 8 November 1292 as a royal town on the land of Kraków.

Area 57,6 km²
Population 79 985

Tourist attractions

  • The Galician Town - one of the most attractive places on the tourist map of Malopolska, and it is worth seeing it to go back in time and experience the atmosphere of the Galician province at the turn of the 19th and 20th centuries.

  • Ethnographic Park - the open-air museum is located next to the Galician Town. It is the largest open-air museum in the Małopolska region and presents wooden architecture and folk traditions of former ethnographic groups.
